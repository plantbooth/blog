Digitalis purpurea 'Dalmatian White' (Dalmatian Series)

Foxglove
A hedgerow plant, foxgloves give a vertical focus to a cottage garden. The pure-white flowers that form on the upright branching stems, have a pretty freckling of plum coloured markings in their throat. These plants are mainly short-lived perennials, although if happy they will produce plenty of new sideshoots and self-seed freely.

Cranesbill
This hardy geranium provides super ground cover growing up to 30cm in height. The violet-blue flowers are rounded with 5 petals. After the first flowers, cut back to the ground to generate a second round of flowering late summer.

Lavandula angustifolia 'Hidcote'

Lavender
Dense spikes of fragrant, deep violet summer flowers above slender, aromatic, silvery-grey leaves. This has got to be a cottage-garden must-have plant. It's great for the edge of a border so the aromatic foliage perfumes the air as you brush your hand against it. The flower-spikes are highly attractive to bees and other nectar-loving insects.
